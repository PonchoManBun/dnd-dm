# 07 — Item Cards

## Overview

Every item in TWW is represented as an **item card** — a popup panel showing the item's pixel art icon, name, stats, rarity border, weight, and description. Item cards appear when loot is found, when inspecting inventory, and during shop transactions.

## Rarity Tiers

Items follow D&D 5e rarity with corresponding border colors:

| Rarity | Border Color | Drop Frequency |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Common** | Gray | Frequent |
| **Uncommon** | Green | Moderate |
| **Rare** | Blue | Infrequent |
| **Very Rare** | Purple | Scarce |
| **Legendary** | Orange/Gold | Extremely rare |

All item stats are **fixed** — no random rolls on magical properties. A *+1 Longsword* is always a *+1 Longsword*.

## Loot Flow

1. DM narrates the discovery: *"Behind the skeleton's ribcage, something glints."*
2. Item card pops up with full details.
3. Player chooses: take (added to inventory) or leave.

## Currency

- **Gold pieces (gp)** — Primary currency. Used at shops, inns, and for services.
- **Gems & valuables** — Found as loot. Must be sold at shops for gold. Value determined by DM.

## Map as Item

The world map is a physical item. Players start without one. If found or purchased, the map item card opens the overworld travel view. Lose the map (death, theft, trade) and you navigate blind — the DM describes directions verbally.

## Encumbrance

Carrying capacity = STR × 15 lbs. Every item has a weight. Exceeding capacity applies movement penalties. The DM warns when approaching the limit.

## Shops

Shop transactions are NPC conversations. Prices are DM-set. Haggling is possible — Charisma checks and roleplay affect outcomes.
