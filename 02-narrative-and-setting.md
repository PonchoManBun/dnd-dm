# 02 — Narrative & Setting

## World Overview

TWW takes place in an original dark-comedy fantasy world. The setting is not a licensed D&D world — it is generated and maintained entirely by the AI Dungeon Master, using D&D 5e rules as its mechanical backbone. The world is grim, absurd, and full of creatures that would rather eat you than talk to you (though some will do both).

## The Welcome Wench

The tavern called **The Welcome Wench** is the hub of the game. It is the only safe place in the world — a ramshackle inn at a crossroads where adventurers gather, drink, brag about dungeons they barely survived, and mourn the ones who didn't. The tavern persists across character deaths. Its patrons remember fallen heroes. Its quest board refreshes with new dangers.

## Tone

- **Dark with gallows humor.** The world is lethal, but the narration finds comedy in the horror. A mimic doesn't just eat you — the DM describes the indignity.
- **Grounded absurdity.** Magic exists, but people still complain about taxes. Dragons hoard gold, but also grudges.
- **Player agency within chaos.** The world doesn't care about the player. It reacts to them.

## Lore Generation

The AI DM generates all lore on the fly: town histories, faction rivalries, NPC backstories, dungeon origins. Lore is seeded from 2–3 faction archetypes at world creation and grows organically through play. There are no pre-written story arcs — every narrative thread is emergent.

## Narrative Techniques

The DM uses tabletop storytelling methods: **node-based design** for locations, **threat clocks** for escalation, the **three-clue rule** for mysteries, **bangs** for dramatic moments, and **"Yes, and…" / "No, but…"** improv logic for player creativity.
