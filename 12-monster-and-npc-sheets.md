# 12 — Monster & NPC Sheets

## Monster Stat Blocks

Monsters follow D&D 5e SRD stat blocks. The AI DM loads the relevant SRD markdown file when a monster is encountered and uses it to resolve all combat mechanics.

### Stat Block Fields

- **AC, HP, Speed** — Core combat stats
- **Ability Scores** — STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA
- **Saving Throws & Skills** — Proficient saves and skills
- **Damage Resistances/Immunities** — Elemental and physical
- **Senses** — Darkvision, blindsight, passive Perception
- **Challenge Rating (CR)** — Determines XP award
- **Actions** — Attacks, special abilities, spellcasting
- **Legendary Actions** — For boss-tier monsters

## Social Intelligence

Not all monsters are mindless. The DM applies a social intelligence rule:

- **Intelligent creatures** (INT 6+) — Can talk, negotiate, surrender, flee, set ambushes, and have goals. The DM runs them as characters.
- **Mindless creatures** (INT 5 or below) — Always fight. No negotiation. Predictable behavior.

This creates a spectrum: a goblin might beg for its life, a dragon might parley, but an ooze just attacks.

## NPC Sheets

NPCs are more than stat blocks. Each NPC tracked by the multi-agent system has:

- **Identity** — Name, race, class/occupation, appearance
- **Personality** — Traits, ideals, bonds, flaws
- **Knowledge** — What they know about the world, the player, and recent events
- **Goals** — What they want (short-term and long-term)
- **Faction** — Allegiance and standing
- **Disposition** — Current attitude toward the player (hostile → friendly spectrum)

## Behavior in Combat

The DM runs monsters tactically based on their intelligence. Smart enemies flank, focus fire on weak targets, and retreat when losing. Beasts act on instinct. Undead follow simple orders.
