# 08 — Permadeath & Tavern Hub

## Permadeath

Death is permanent. When a character reaches 0 HP, they get one **last stand** — a single final action before dying. This could be a desperate attack, a shouted warning, a thrown potion, or anything the player can justify. The DM adjudicates.

### Death Flow

1. **0 HP** — DM announces the mortal blow.
2. **Last stand** — Player declares one final action. DM resolves it.
3. **Death narration** — The DM narrates the character's death with appropriate gravity (and dark humor).
4. **Eulogy** — A short DM-written tribute to the fallen character.
5. **World log** — The character's deeds are recorded in the world's persistent history.
6. **New character** — Player returns to character creation. The tavern awaits a new adventurer.

### No Save-Scumming

Saves are **hybrid**: the server continuously persists game state, but save data is deleted when a session resumes. There is no manual save, no reload, no branching. What happens, happens.

## The Tavern Hub

The Welcome Wench is the persistent home base. It is a full pixel art scene — the player walks around, interacts with objects, and talks to NPCs.

### Tavern Features

- **Bar** — Talk to the bartender. Buy drinks. Hear rumors.
- **Quest board** — Browse available quests (AI-generated).
- **Shop** — Buy and sell equipment from the resident merchant.
- **Rooms** — Rest (long rest) to recover HP, spell slots, and abilities.
- **NPCs** — Rotating cast of patrons with dynamic dialogue and personality.
- **Memorial wall** — Names and deeds of fallen characters from previous runs.

### World Memory

NPCs remember previous characters. The bartender might say: *"You look like that half-orc who died in the Ashmaw Caves last week. Try not to follow her example."* Death feeds the world's narrative. Legacy is the only meta-progression.
